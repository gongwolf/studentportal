Program Descriptions and Requirements

Carefully review the brief program descriptions and requirements before submitting an application. For more detailed information on the program, visit nmamp.nmsu.edu.


Applications with the BLUE TAB, such as Community College Professional Development Workshop Stipend, Summer Community College Opportunity for Research Experience (SCCORE) Program, and Transfer Scholarship are only available for students enrolled in a New Mexico Community College.


Applications with the GREEN TAB, such as MESA Scholarship, International Research and Education Participation (IREP) and Undergraduate Research Scholars (URS) are available to students that have been accepted/planned or enrolled in a New Mexico 4-year Institution.


Community College Professional Development Workshop Stipend

New Mexico AMP Community College Professional Development Workshop Stipend (Community College)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

Community College Professional Development Workshop Stipends are awarded to a New Mexico community college student to attend and participate in the New Mexico AMP Student Research Conference at the Las Cruces Convention Center adjacent to the New Mexico State University Campus, in October. The New Mexico AMP program also will pay your conference registration fee.

You must attend the entire conference from 3:00 p.m. Thursday through noon on Saturday — failure to do so may disqualify you from receiving the stipend. Please contact us if you have questions.

NOTE: Students are eligible to receive this stipend only twice — students who have already received a second award are welcome to attend but only their registration will be paid.

What are the requirements to receive a stipend?

  • Hispanic/Latino, American Indian/Alaskan Native, African American/Black, Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander, other underrepresented minority as defined by the National Science Foundation.
  • Currently enrolled full time or part-time at a New Mexico community college.
  • A major in a science, technology, engineering or math (STEM) discipline.
  • A minimum 2.7 GPA.
  • Transcripts must provide evidence of STEM related pre-transfer coursework.
  • Submission of a completed application form, with the one-page essay and a current unofficial transcript.
  • Attend and actively participate all three days of the conference.

Application Requirements:

  • Application with the essay questions
    • Complete the essay questions with thoughtful and thorough responses
  • Community College Transcripts
    • Cumulative 2.7 GPA or greater
    • Evaluated to help access academic progress and intent (e.g., evidence of success in math, science, English, and pre-engineering course)
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
Summer Community College Opportunity for Research Experience

New Mexico AMP Summer Community College Opportunity for Research Experience (SCCORE) (Community College)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

Targeted to community college students in all STEM fields, the annual Summer Community College Opportunity for Research Experience (SCCORE) program supports pre-transfer community college students to serve as research assistants to university faculty mentors in their related fields in the summer. Participating students live on the university campus, attend a credit-bearing class, and receive professional development training.

For more information: https://nmamp.nmsu.edu/sccore/

Application Requirements:

  • Application with the questionnaire
    • Complete the questions
    • Identify two top 4-year institutions of where you want to attend the SCCORE Program
  • Unofficial transcript (most recent)
  • Medical Emergency Care Authorization Form
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
Transfer Scholarship

New Mexico AMP Transfer Scholarship (Community College)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

The Transfer Scholarship is available to students transferring from a 2-year to a 4-year institution to pursue a degree in a STEM discipline. The scholarship is awarded to community college students the first semester at the four-year New Mexico Institution.

For more information: https://nmamp.nmsu.edu/transfer-scholarship/

Application Requirements:

  • Application with the essay questions
    • Complete the essay questions with thoughtful and thorough responses
    • Identify the 4-year institution you are transferring to
  • Letter of Recommendation (1 letter required)
    • Letter of recommendation from a community college instructor or a New Mexico AMP institutional coordinator
  • Community College Transcripts
    • Cumulative 2.5 GPA or greater at the time of transfer
    • Evaluated to help access academic progress and intent (e.g., evidence of success in math, science, English, and pre-engineering course)
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
MESA Scholarship

New Mexico AMP MESA Scholarship (4-year institution)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

The New Mexico MESA scholarship is available to students who have participated in the New Mexico MESA program in high school. Applicants must demonstrate an intent to complete a B.S. degree in a STEM field at a 4-year institution and have a cumulative 3.0 GPA or higher.

Application Requirements:

  • Application with the essay questions
    • Complete the essay questions with thoughtful and thorough responses
    • Identify the 4-year institution you are planning to attend
  • High School Transcripts
    • Cumulative 3.0 GPA (last graded semester)
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
Undergraduate Research Scholars (URS)

New Mexico AMP Undergraduate Research Scholars (URS) (4-year institution)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

Undergraduate Research Scholars (URS) serve as apprentice researchers under the supervision of a faculty mentor. Students establish a working schedule with their mentors and are expected to work a minimum of 10 hours per week during the academic year (fall and spring semesters) and 20 hours per week during the summer. Applications must include a focused research project proposal. Students are eligible to participate in the URS program until they graduate; however, students must complete a reapplication process at the end of each semester to update eligibility requirements and research activities and goals.

For more information: https://nmamp.nmsu.edu/undergraduate-research-scholars-urs/

Application Requirements:

  • Application
  • Unofficial transcripts (current)
  • Project Proposal
  • Mentor/Student Goals
  • Faculty Mentor Information
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
International Research and Education Participation (IREP)

New Mexico AMP International Research and Education Participation (IREP) (4-year institution)

Application deadline:

Brief Description:

New Mexico AMP has designated funding to support students participating in international research experiences. To qualify for this support, students must work with their research mentors (domestic and international) to plan the project and determine anticipated expenses. Many students begin this process with the support of their faculty mentor, who may have funding as well as international collaborators through his or her research program. Students are expected to leverage funding from multiple sources to support their international experience. The New Mexico AMP Institutional Coordinator is available to assist students to identify funding opportunities and to support the planning process.

For more information: https://nmamp.nmsu.edu/international-research-education-participation-irep/

Funding Requirements/Guidelines:

  • Present at the New Mexico AMP Conference (typically late September/early October).
  • You may be asked to complete an online survey upon your return.
  • You may be asked to participate in a Focus Group to share your experience and help us to evaluate success of the IREP Scholars leveraging funding opportunity.
  • You may be asked to complete a "Trip Report" (a form that asks you information on your experience & knowledge gained from your trip.
  • This is not a scholarship. Approved “out of pocket” expenses will only be reimbursed with receipts.

Application Requirements:

  • A completed International Research and Education Participation (IREP Scholars) Funding Request Application
  • A completed Budget Worksheet
  • A 250 word Project Abstract for the summer project
  • Unofficial transcripts (current)
  • Faculty Mentor Information
  • Student Agreement and Disclosure Form (filled out as part of your profile)
